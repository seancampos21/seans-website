Into the Wild: A Mountain Lion Encounter

The Encounter

It was just another day hiking deep in the Santa Cruz Mountains. The sun was low, filtering through the redwoods like golden smoke. The forest was quiet — not eerie, just still in that way nature sometimes gets when it’s holding its breath.

That’s when I saw it.

A mountain lion. Not in the distance, not through binoculars — but there, maybe 30 feet away, standing stone-still on the trail ahead. It wasn’t crouched or aggressive. Just watching. Like it had been there the whole time, waiting for me to notice it.

I froze. My breath got shallow. Every instinct in me screamed to run, but something deeper — maybe ancient — told me to stay still. Don’t look it in the eye. Don’t turn your back.

For what felt like a minute, we just existed there — two animals in the forest. It eventually turned, padded silently into the trees, and disappeared as if it had never been there.

What It Taught Me

People talk about being humbled by nature, but this was different. I didn’t feel small — I felt real. Awake. The kind of moment that burns itself into your brain and changes your wiring.

Since that day, the wilderness hasn’t just been scenic. It’s alive — and it watches back.
